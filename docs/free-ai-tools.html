Strategies for Maximising Free Tiers

1. Spread your prompts across tools

Don't rely on a single tool. By rotating between ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, and Meta AI, you effectively get dozens of free prompts per day rather than being stuck with one tool's limit.

2. Make every prompt count

Use the RTCF framework (Role, Task, Context, Format) to write specific, detailed prompts. One well-crafted prompt is worth more than five vague ones.

3. Use NotebookLM as your workhorse

For assignment research, NotebookLM is your best friend. Upload your readings, lecture notes, and papers. It answers from YOUR sources only - no hallucination, generous limits, and perfect for literature review work.

4. Plan before you prompt

Before opening an AI tool, write down what you actually need. Draft your prompt in a text editor first. This saves wasted prompts on poorly worded requests.

Local AI - Unlimited and Private

If you want unlimited AI access with complete privacy (nothing leaves your laptop), you can run AI models locally:

Msty

Desktop app with a friendly interface. Runs Llama, Mistral, and other models locally. Can also connect to cloud APIs. Good for beginners.

msty.app

Ollama

Command-line tool for running models locally. More technical but very powerful. Download models with a single command. Pairs well with Msty as a backend.

ollama.com

What do you need to run local AI?

A laptop with at least 8GB of RAM can run smaller models (7-8 billion parameters). For better quality, 16GB+ RAM is recommended. Both Msty and Ollama work on Mac, Windows, and Linux. No internet connection required once the model is downloaded.

Recommended Workflow

  1. Start with NotebookLM - upload your sources, do grounded research
  2. Use a chatbot for brainstorming - try reverse prompting or Board of Directors techniques
  3. Rotate tools when you hit limits - switch from ChatGPT to Claude to Grok
  4. VET everything - Verify, Explain, Test before using any AI output
  5. Consider local AI - if you need unlimited access or handle sensitive data

The Billboard Rule

Before sharing anything with AI, ask: "Would I be comfortable if this appeared on a billboard?" Never share passwords, personal data about others, or confidential information. This applies to all tools - cloud and local.
